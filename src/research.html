Research Projects & Internships

Effect of Health & Wellness Centers under Ayushman Bharat on Out Of Pocket Expenditure of Geriatric Population: A Multicenter Study

Summer, 2024
Supervisor: Prof. Indranil Mukhopadhyay

Under Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Govt. of India.

Assessed the impact of Health & Wellness Centers (HWCs) on healthcare expenses for the elderly across 3 Indian states (Rajasthan, Tripura, Karnataka) under the Ayushman Bharat initiative. Analyzed data from over 9000 elderly individuals, focusing on out-of-pocket expenditure (OOPE) by gender, age, village type, and insurance status.

Macroeconomic Data Analysis & Financial Forecasting

Fall, 2023
Supervisor: Subhashis Raha

Analyzed macroeconomic and financial market data to develop forecasting models using time-series econometrics (ARIMA, VAR). Processed and structured data from over 7500 financial transactions, improving predictive accuracy by 15%. Developed budgeting frameworks that enhanced financial planning, leading to a projected 10% reduction in operational costs.

Income Inequality and Economic Growth in India

Summer, 2023
Supervisor: Prof. S. Patra

Analyzed the effects of income inequality on GDP growth, examining regional disparities and their economic consequences over 2 decades. Employed regression analysis on a dataset of 5000+ data points from Indian economic surveys (2000-2020) using Advanced econometric modeling (OLS, panel data regression), statistical software (Stata, R).

Demographic Trends and Issues Analysis in India

Summer, 2022
Supervisor: Prof. N. Chakraborty

Analyzed demographic shifts over the past 30 years, focusing on population growth, age distribution, & urbanization. Examined over 10,000 data points from national census reports (1990-2020), applying statistical modeling to forecast future demographic impacts.

Indian Banking Sector Reforms: Policies and Performance Analysis

Summer, 2021
Supervisor: Prof. S. Patra

Evaluated the impact of major banking reforms on the performance metrics of Indian banks over the past 25 years. Analyzed a dataset of 8000+ data points from RBI reports (1995-2020), using statistical models to measure reform outcomes.
