Vizag-Araku Trip (2022)

A journey through beaches, hills, and valleys

In 2022, I set off on a much-awaited trip with my five closest school friends‚Äîa journey that would take us from the serene beaches of Visakhapatnam (Vizag) to the breathtaking landscapes of Araku Valley. It was a mix of nostalgia, adventure, and pure joy as we reunited after years and made unforgettable memories.

Our trip started in Visakhapatnam, a city known for its golden beaches, rich naval history, and scenic hills. The first thing we did was visit RK Beach, where we ran towards the waves like excited kids, taking in the refreshing sea breeze. It was a carefree moment that reminded us of our school days.

One of the highlights was the INS Kursura Submarine Museum, where we got a rare look inside an actual decommissioned Indian Navy submarine. Walking through its narrow passages and seeing the control rooms gave us immense respect for the naval officers who once operated it.

We also visited Kailasagiri, a hilltop park offering a panoramic view of Vizag. Taking the ropeway up to the top, we were mesmerized by the sunset over the Bay of Bengal‚Äîone of the most peaceful sights I've ever seen.

The next morning, we boarded the famous Vizag-Araku train, and it turned out to be one of the most scenic train journeys of our lives. As the train slowly climbed through lush green forests, waterfalls, and mist-covered hills, we passed through 58 tunnels and 84 bridges‚Äîeach one offering a postcard-perfect view.

We spent most of the journey sticking our heads out of the windows, laughing, and clicking endless photos. The excitement in our group was contagious!

‚òï Exploring Araku ‚Äì Coffee, Caves & Culture

Araku Valley welcomed us with rolling hills, coffee plantations, and cool mountain air. We visited a tribal coffee museum where we tasted freshly brewed organic Araku coffee, and it was easily one of the best coffees I've ever had.

Our next stop was the Borra Caves, an ancient limestone cave system with massive stalactites and stalagmites. Walking inside, we felt like explorers uncovering a hidden world. The caves were illuminated in colorful lights, adding to their mystical aura.

That night, we sat around a bonfire, sharing old stories, laughing until our stomachs hurt, and realizing how precious our friendship was. This trip was more than just sightseeing‚Äîit was about cherishing bonds, reliving childhood, and creating new memories.
